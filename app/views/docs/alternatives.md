# Alternatives to jam.coop

jam.coop isn't the only place you can upload your music and sell directly to your supporters. We're building something open-source, UK based, democratic, friendly and hopeful - and without external funding. We hope you enjoy your time here, but we don't want to lock you in!

Here are some alternatives you may want to consider

## Co-operative alternatives

- [**Mirlo**](https://mirlo.space/) is an open-source, co-operatively owned online music platform based in the US. We're actively working with them to find ways to support each other (in the [spirit of co-operation](https://www.uk.coop/understanding-co-ops/what-co-op/co-op-values-and-principles)).
- [**Subvert**](https://www.subvert.fm/) is a relative newcomer to the co-operative music platform space. They're a closed source, US-based platform co-operative with a growing base of [musicians](https://www.subvert.fm/discover/artists) and [funders](https://www.subvert.fm/docs/funding-history-investor-list).

## Other alternatives

- [**Ampwall**](https://ampwall.com/) is a US public benefit company created by lifelong DIY musicians.
- [**Bandcamp**](https://bandcamp.com/) is the largest direct-to-supporter platform for musicians. Based in the US with a huge roster of musicians they offer a large community and many great features. They are, however, privately owned and for-profit. Bandcamp has changed ownership in the past and actively resisted the formation of the [Bandcamp United](https://www.bandcampunited.org/faq) workers' union. This and our worry for the eventual [platform decay](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Enshittification) of Bandcamp led us, in 2023, to start work on jam.coop.
- [**Bandwagon**](https://bandwagon.fm/) is a social music sharing service for the Fediverse.
- [**Faircamp**](https://faircamp.org/) is an open source tool that  allows you to create your own website for your music and sell directly to your supporters.
